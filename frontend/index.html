FreshGuard

Catch decay a day earlier. Sell it, don't bin it.

A two stage vision pipeline grades every fruit & veg Fresh / Sell soon / Reject in real time, then an LSTM forecasts spoilage a week out and recovers the margin that used to rot on the shelf.

The problem

Good food rots on the shelf.

Freshness is checked by eye, once or twice a shift. By the time someone flags decay, the window to sell it at a markdown is already gone, and the item is spoiling everything next to it.

The stakes · why it matters

€100k a year, per store.

Produce is the worst shrink category in the store, up to 12% lost. A typical store bins €75k to €120k of produce a year; on 2 to 3% grocery margins, every €1 saved is worth €30 to €50 of revenue.

Three architectures · one verdict

We earned 95.6%.

Same data, three models: a plain neural net (22.2%), a CNN from scratch (73.3%), then transfer learning. The pipeline grades each item Fresh / Sell soon / Reject at 0.99 AUC, with a confidence gate that abstains when unsure.

It learns the calendar

+42.7% sharper forecasts.

An LSTM reads 28 days of history and the Spanish retail calendar to forecast spoilage a week out, beating a naive baseline by 42.7% MAE. Every scan you make sharpens the next forecast: the data flywheel.

Compliant by design

Spain Law 1/2025.

The law prohibits binning edible produce and mandates discounting or donating items nearing expiry. Loose produce has no expiry date, so visual condition is the only auditable signal, and FreshGuard logs every call.

Model report

Three architectures, same data, the ablation that earned transfer learning.

How each architecture sees the image live signal flow · why transfer learning won
ANNfully-connected22.2%

Pixels are flattened and wired to every neuron. Spatial structure is destroyed, it can't tell where decay is, only that the numbers changed.

CNNconvolution73.3%

A kernel slides across the image building feature maps, edges, then texture, then rot. Geometry is preserved, so it learns where.

RNNLSTM · memory+42.7%

A hidden state carries memory across days of flagged-item history → forecasts next week's spoilage. The grader's data, in time.
